Pokémon Go

Pokémon Go (PoGo) is a fun way to get out and explore your city with a fun augmented reality game to help you along. PoGo is a mobile game compatible with Android and iOS that uses your device's GPS to track your location, allowing the game to follow you around to search for Pokémon, PokéStops, and Gyms.

Pokémon Available to Capture

Currently, PoGo allows you to capture any Pokémon up to and including Generation 3 (there are around 400 available to capture!). The more rare breeds of pokemon can only be acquired by teaming up with others to complete raids in which you use your Pokémon to take down and possibly capture the rare beast.

Pokéstops

Pokéstops are PoGo's representation of smaller landmarks marked in your city. While walking, you will encounter several Pokéstops which you can spin for valuable resources such as pokéballs (used to capture Pokémon) and battling supplies such as potions and revives. There is also a rare chance to obtain a special evolution item which will help certain Pokémon reach their final and most powerful stage. It is important to have strong Pokémon on your team so you can compete with other trainers in local Gyms.

Gyms

Pokémon Gyms are PoGo's representation of larger landmarks marked in your city. At these gyms, trainers from three different teams can battle with each other to claim that particular gym as their own... until another team takes it down. Only trainers with strong Pokémon can compete in these gyms as a strong defence from another team will withstand the assault from a much weaker opponent.
