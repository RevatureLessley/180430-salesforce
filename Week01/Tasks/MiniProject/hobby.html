My Hobbies

I am a playing card collector and specialize in the different patterns of playing cards used around the world. (A pattern is a basic design for the fronts of a deck of cards. The English pattern, also called the Anglo-American pattern or the international pattern, is the most widespread as well as the best-known in the United States. Shown below are some cards from the Paris pattern.) I started collecting in earnest in 2009 and now have over 100 decks from the United States, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

Patterns are associated with suit systems. The familiar spades, hearts, diamonds, and clubs belong to the French suit system. Three others are in widespread use:

  1. Latin suit system: Swords, cups, coins, and batons. There are two major subtypes:
    1. Spanish suit system: The swords and batons are straight and do not cross.
    2. Italian suit system: The swords and batons cross; the swords are curved, and in the numeral cards this is often the easiest way to tell them apart from the batons.
  2. German suit system: Leaves, hearts, bells, acorns
  3. Swiss German system: Shields, flowers, bells, acorns
