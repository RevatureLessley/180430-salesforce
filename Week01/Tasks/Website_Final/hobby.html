Some DOs and DONTs for any of you that hope to do some traveling around Europe:

DO:

  • Pack outlet converters. You don't want to make it through an 8 hour flight and not be able to charge your phone once you get there.
  • Stay in hostels if you're on a budget. They're super cheap and you'll meet lots of people from all over the world. They often serve free breakfast or offer things like free walking tours or cheap bar crawls. Do your research and you can save a few bucks and hit all the major landmarks during your stay.
  • Learn at least a little bit of the local language. You should be able to order food and ask for the check, and it doesn't hurt to be able to ask for directions.
  • Call your bank and put a travel notice on your card. You don't want to have your card declined and then have to pay for international minutes to call your bank.
  • Drink lots of water, you'll be walking a lot. Like a lot.

DON'T:

  • Pack more than one carry-on bag. Even if your international flight checks your bag for free, every cheap airline with the continent will charge you for it. Plus, you're going to be dragging your stuff everywhere, you don't want it to be too heavy.
  • Forget your headphones. You will regret it when you're on a long flight or stuck on public transit.
  • Forget to print/download your plane ticket. Do both just to be safe.
  • Be rude. You don't have to be fluent in the language to be polite.
